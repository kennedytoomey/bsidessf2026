The Silent Threat

An npm package configured to upgrade automatically. Everything is working great. Then a new version is released. No one thinks twice about it.

This is the story of what happens when that silent assumption breaks at scale.

03 · Architecture
"Chain of Fools"

The Architecture of Dependencies

Dependencies are all interconnected, each relying on tens of transitive dependencies. A single point of failure doesn't stay contained. It propagates.

Understanding the blast radius before the incident is the only way to reason about it during one.

04 · Incident Response
"Manic Monday"

Responding When It's Already on Fire

What happened? New information is being released constantly. Stay up to date.

Are you affected? Does an SBOM exist? Is it current? This is the moment you find out if your inventory is real.

Stay organized.

  • Create a centralized tracking sheet immediately
  • Assign clear responsibilities so nothing falls between teams

10 · Proactive Defense
"You Can't Always Get What You Want"

Before the Incident Happens

Guiding principles: Least Privilege and Defense in Depth. The goal is a reduced attack surface — not perfect security.

Fine-Grained Access Tokens (FGAT) — limit the blast radius of any single compromised credential
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) — on every account with publishing rights
Minimum package age requirements — don't auto-upgrade to a package published five minutes ago

11 · Post-Mortem
"Stronger (What Doesn't Kill You)"

The Debrief

What went well? What didn't?

  • Did you have an easy way to find vulnerable versions across your stack?
  • Did you face friction from supporting teams during the response?
  • Did you have a communications plan in place — or were you making it up?

What can be improved for next time? Make a concrete plan to fix anything that didn't go well. Before the next incident starts.

12 · Documentation
"Why Do You Write Like You're Running Out of Time?"

Making It Repeatable

  • Document the response efforts — every step, every decision, every escalation
  • Create templates for communications — so the next incident doesn't start from a blank page
  • Write SOPs on what to do in similar situations
  • Communicate results with stakeholders and customers — close the loop
